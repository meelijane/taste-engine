# Quiet Currents — an algorithmic philosophy

A taste is not a fixed point; it is a *drift*. The things we love pull on us like
unseen forces, and the path we trace between them is the shape of who we are. **Quiet
Currents** renders that invisible pull: a flow field of slow-moving particles, each one
a small preference following the gradient of an attention that never quite settles.

The field is woven from layered periodic functions — sines and cosines breathing across
the plane at incommensurate frequencies, so the current never repeats and never jolts.
Particles are released into it and simply *obey*: at each step they read the local angle
and lean into it, laying down a faint luminous trace before the dark slowly reclaims
them. No particle is important; the image is the accumulation, the after-image of
thousands of small surrenders. Order is never imposed — it precipitates, the way a
sensibility precipitates out of a hundred half-conscious choices.

Restraint is the whole discipline. This is a *backdrop*, not a performance: it must live
beneath language and never compete with it. The strokes are kept near the threshold of
perception — present as atmosphere, absent as subject. A reader should feel the page is
quietly alive without ever being able to say why. Every alpha value, every step length,
every frequency ratio is the product of patient calibration, tuned until the motion
reads as a slow exhale rather than a screensaver.

Colour is singular and borrowed. The current takes the room's own accent — amber for
comedy, violet for music, blue for television, crimson for film, emerald for books — and
lets that single hue glow against the near-black, so the backdrop belongs to its section
without being told to. One colour, many temperaments.

Reproducibility is part of the calm: a seed fixes the release pattern, so the same room
always breathes the same way. And the system yields to the reader — when motion is
unwelcome it settles instantly into a single still frame, the current frozen mid-drift,
no less composed for having stopped.

The result should feel less *drawn* than *weathered*: a surface that an algorithm tended
with care across countless iterations until the chaos finally learned to whisper.
